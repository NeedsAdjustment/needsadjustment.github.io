idk

It's a three day weekend in a couple of days
Three days of lonely thinking about the grave
And I've finally stopped talking in my dreamless sleep
About good weather days where we might meet

My usual ride's out of town again, and it's too
dark to walk all the way back home again, but I know you
won't sleep easy if I'm not blowing up your phone
so just let me go anyway, okay?

Don't pretend like you know me, I was born feeling lonely
It's not the first time you've invited me over, and it's
Almost too early to start drinking hard liquor but
whatever gets you to stop ever touching my fingers

And it's too late for this stupid ass poem to rhyme,
And I don't care that I haven't replied to your message tonight
And if my phone says "salut" again I swear I'll stop
playing Widowmaker so please just give me a break.

The Truth

All that is true, we preach
as we lie in a bed of lies.
Twisting snakes know the way
to a dark crown, but colder still
is that bright gleam of truth.

Cold fire and searing light
would burn even the saints away
to glittering dust in the sun.
And I'm no saint, so how should
that white ray cleanse me?

And twice I spoke, twice a lie
on a pale and faded morning
where the singing birds were silent.
So perhaps my dark crown might stop
the advance of the sun.

Or maybe as we plummet from Earth
and the beat of drums finally ends
a white wing might shroud us
and burn away. But the remnants
shall be our parachutes.

A Raven

You may not leave
the battlefield grim
the light in your eyes dim
you must stay

or so they say.

Do not think it fine
to let wings fly dripping
to the sun above the world
you must stay

or so they say.

Claim the right
to slip into endless night
and taste the fire
of endless light

that's what they say.

But if I decide to fly
breathless and tired
and speak to a raven
would he take me?

or would I crumple to the ground?

A Quiet

There is a quiet in things unsaid;
a hole in the world where dark things spread
their wings to fly and gnaw and tear
and rip at what helps us hold dear.

Speak twice your heart, or not at all,
but you must think on what enthralls
your tired soul never laid bare
and why you must not lose those cares.

or they'll slip away, one fateful night -
all those hidden, dark delights.
Twixt your beaten, bloody fingers
naught but soft grey dust will linger.

(and past the corners of your mind,
in the reflection of faded eyes,
you'll see a proud grey dragon await
his twice-forgotten final fate.)

Tears

but Why, she asks
no-one knows

Why the mountains must
crumble into dust.

Why the trees may crack and
die, parched by the sun

while we water them
gently, with tears.

Ice

One day
the ice will break
and I'll fall into your arms
or into the empty embrace
of the end

(don't wait too long)

Don't Wait

time to go out
the wide world awaits
fools dance in the wind
stones break against the shore
don't wait
or you will bend to the ground
and leave your head in the air
